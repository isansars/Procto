1. Executive Summary
The company currently procures goods and services across multiple branches using informal, disconnected channels — WhatsApp messages, emails, spreadsheets, and verbal approvals. This creates blind spots at every step: procurement teams cannot confirm whether a request is genuinely approved, purchase orders may not reflect what was actually approved, and warehouses have no systematic way to record what was received against what was ordered.

This PRD defines an ERP Procurement module that digitizes and connects the full process — Purchase Request (PR) → Approval → Purchase Order (PO) → Goods Receipt (GR) — into a single, centralized, auditable system across all branches. The goal is to give every stakeholder (requesters, approvers, procurement officers, warehouse staff, and management) a single source of truth for the status of any request, order, or delivery, at any time.

2. Problem Statement
A company with multiple branches currently manages procurement through manual channels such as WhatsApp, email, spreadsheets, and offline approvals. Employees submit purchase requests informally, approvals are difficult to track, and procurement teams often need to follow up manually to confirm request details, approval status, vendor selection, and delivery progress.

6. End-to-End Process Flow
6.1 Future-State Process (Target)
Requester creates a Purchase Request (PR) specifying branch, department, cost center, items/services, quantities, estimated cost, urgency, and justification.
PR is submitted and routed automatically through the configured approval matrix (may involve 1–3 sequential approval levels depending on value and category).
Each approver reviews the PR and can: Approve, Reject, Request Revision (sends back to requester with comments), or Approve Partially (approve some line items, reject/hold others).
Once fully approved, the PR enters the Procurement Queue, visible to Procurement Officers.
Procurement Officer reviews the approved PR, selects a vendor (from vendor master, or optionally consolidates multiple PRs from different requesters into a single PO for the same vendor), and creates a Purchase Order (PO).
PO is issued to the vendor. Depending on configuration, POs above a threshold require Procurement Manager approval before issuance.
As goods arrive at the branch, Warehouse/Store staff creates a Goods Receipt (GR) against the PO — recording quantity received, condition (good/damaged/rejected), and any discrepancy notes. Multiple GRs can be recorded against a single PO for partial deliveries over time.
The PO is automatically marked Partially Received or Fully Received based on cumulative GR quantities versus ordered quantities. The originating PR is marked Fulfilled once its linked PO(s) are fully received.
At every step, status changes are logged to the audit trail, and relevant stakeholders receive notifications (in-app + email).
Management can view real-time dashboards showing open PRs, pending approvals, open POs, and GR status across all branches at any time — no manual compilation required.

7. Approval Matrix Design
The approval workflow must be configurable, not hard-coded, since approval chains vary by branch, department, and PR value. The System Admin configures an Approval Matrix that the platform uses to automatically route each PR.

7.2 Approval Matrix Rules
Configurable by Admin: Amount thresholds, approver roles, and chain order are all configurable per branch and/or department — not hard-coded.
Sequential by default: Each level must approve before the PR moves to the next level. Parallel approval (all approvers notified simultaneously) is a Phase 2 candidate.
Delegation: Approvers can delegate their approval authority to a designated backup for a defined date range (e.g., while on leave), with the delegation logged in the audit trail.
SLA Escalation: If an approver does not act within a configurable SLA (e.g., 48 hours), the system sends a reminder; after a second SLA breach, the PR is escalated to the next-level approver or the approver&apos;s manager.
Partial Approval: An approver can approve some PR line items and reject/hold others. Approved lines proceed to procurement; rejected lines are returned to the requester with comments.



8. Functional Requirements by Module
8.1 Module: Purchase Request (PR)
PR Header: Requester, branch, department, cost center, date needed, urgency level (Normal / Urgent), justification/notes, attachments (quotes, specs).
PR Line Items: Item/service description, category, quantity, unit of measure, estimated unit price, estimated total, preferred vendor (optional).
Item Catalog Lookup: Requesters can search a shared item catalog to auto-fill item description and last-purchased price, or add a free-text item if not in the catalog.
Draft & Edit: PRs can be saved as drafts and edited freely before submission. Once submitted, edits are locked; the requester must withdraw and resubmit, or wait for a Revision Request from an approver.
Budget Indicator: PR form shows the requester&apos;s department/cost center remaining budget for the period (soft warning in MVP; hard block in Phase 2).
Withdraw: Requester can withdraw a PR any time before it is fully approved.

8.2 Module: Approval Workflow
Approval Inbox: Each approver sees a queue of PRs pending their action, sorted by submission date or urgency.
PR Review View: Approver sees full PR detail, requester history (past PRs), department budget context, and can Approve / Reject / Request Revision / Approve Partially.
Comments Required on Reject/Revision: A comment is mandatory when rejecting or requesting revision, so the requester understands what to fix.
Approval Timeline: Every PR shows a visual timeline of who approved/rejected at each level, with timestamps and comments — visible to the requester and procurement team.
Delegation Settings: Approvers can set a delegate for a date range from their profile settings.

8.3 Module: Vendor & Item Master
Vendor Master: Vendor name, contact info, category, payment terms, status (active/inactive), linked branches.
Item Catalog: Item/service master with category, standard unit of measure, and last purchase price — shared across branches, filterable by branch-specific pricing if applicable.
Vendor Selection at PO Creation: Procurement Officer can select from the vendor master or add a new vendor (subject to Admin approval for the vendor to become active).

8.4 Module: Purchase Order (PO)
PO Creation from Approved PR: Procurement Officer creates a PO by selecting one or more approved PR line items. The system prevents creating a PO from any PR line item that isn&apos;t fully approved.
PR Consolidation: Multiple PRs (from different requesters, same branch or department) requesting the same item/vendor can be consolidated into a single PO for efficiency; each PO line item retains a reference back to its originating PR line item(s).
PR Splitting: A single PR with multiple line items can be split into multiple POs if different vendors are used for different items.
PO Approval (Threshold-Based): POs above a configurable value threshold require Procurement Manager approval before being issued to the vendor.
PO Issuance: Once approved, PO is issued (generates a PDF, sent to vendor via email or download).
PO Amendment: Before any goods receipt is recorded, a PO can be amended (quantity, price) with a reason logged; after partial receipt, amendments are restricted to remaining unreceived quantity.
PO Cancellation: A PO with no goods receipt yet can be cancelled with a reason; the originating PR reverts to Approved status for re-processing.

8.5 Module: Goods Receipt (GR)
GR Creation Against PO: Warehouse/Store staff selects an open PO and records the goods physically received.
Line-Level Recording: For each PO line item: quantity received, condition (Good / Damaged / Rejected), and notes. Photos can be attached as evidence, especially for damaged/rejected goods.
Partial Delivery Support: Multiple GRs can be created against a single PO over time as goods arrive in batches; the system tracks cumulative received quantity vs. ordered quantity per line item.
Over-Delivery Handling: If received quantity exceeds ordered quantity beyond a configurable tolerance (e.g., 5%), the system flags the GR for procurement review before confirming.
Discrepancy Flag: Any GR with damaged, rejected, or under/over quantity is automatically flagged and visible on the procurement and management dashboards for follow-up (e.g., vendor claim, replacement).
Auto PO/PR Status Update: Once cumulative received quantity equals ordered quantity (within tolerance) for all PO lines, the PO is marked Fully Received and the originating PR is marked Fulfilled.

8.6 Module: Spend Visibility & Reporting
Branch/Department Spend Dashboard: Real-time view of committed spend (approved PRs + open POs) vs. budget, by branch and department, for a selected period.
Open Items Dashboard (Management): Live counts and drill-down lists of: Open PRs, Pending Approvals (by approver/level), Open POs, Goods Receipt status (on-time, overdue, discrepancy-flagged).
Aging Report: PRs pending approval beyond SLA, POs open beyond expected delivery date, highlighted for follow-up.
Export: All reports exportable to Excel/CSV for further analysis or external reporting.

8.7 Module: Audit Trail
Full Event Log: Every status change on a PR, PO, or GR (who, what changed, from/to status, timestamp, comments) is logged immutably.
Searchable & Filterable: Audit log searchable by entity ID, user, date range, branch, or action type — accessible to Admin and Management roles.
Non-Editable: Audit log entries cannot be edited or deleted by any user, including Admins — only appended to.

11. User Stories
User stories follow the format: As a [role], I want to [need], So I can [objective]. Acceptance criteria use GIVEN / WHEN / THEN.

US-01: Creating and Submitting a Purchase Request
As a employee at a branch, I want to create and submit a purchase request with clear item details and justification, So I can get the items I need through a proper, trackable process instead of messaging someone informally.
This feature will be used on the PR Creation screen, the primary entry point for all procurement activity.

AC#1 PR Draft and Submission
GIVEN the requester is on the Create Purchase Request screen
WHEN they fill in branch, department, at least one line item, and tap &apos;Submit&apos;
THEN the PR is created with status &apos;Pending Approval&apos; and routed to the first approver per the Approval Matrix
WHEN the requester taps &apos;Save as Draft&apos; instead
THEN the PR is saved with status &apos;Draft&apos;, editable, and not yet routed to any approver

AC#2 Budget Visibility at Submission
GIVEN the requester has selected a department and cost center
WHEN the PR form is rendered
THEN the remaining budget for that department/cost center for the current period is displayed
WHEN the PR total would exceed the remaining budget
THEN a warning banner is shown, but submission is still allowed (MVP behavior)

AC#3 Withdraw Before Full Approval
GIVEN a submitted PR has not yet completed all approval levels
WHEN the requester taps &apos;Withdraw&apos;
THEN the PR status changes to &apos;Withdrawn&apos; and is removed from any pending approver&apos;s queue

US-02: Approving or Rejecting a Purchase Request
As a department approver or branch manager, I want to review and act on purchase requests assigned to me, So I can control what gets purchased in my area without needing to chase details manually.
This feature will be used on the Approval Inbox and PR Review screens.

AC#1 Approve / Reject / Request Revision
GIVEN a PR is pending the approver&apos;s action
WHEN the approver taps &apos;Approve&apos;
THEN the PR advances to the next approval level, or to &apos;Approved&apos; status if this was the final level
WHEN the approver taps &apos;Reject&apos; or &apos;Request Revision&apos;
THEN a comment field is required before the action can be confirmed, and the PR returns to the requester with that comment

AC#2 Partial Line-Item Approval
GIVEN a PR has multiple line items
WHEN the approver approves some line items and rejects others
THEN approved line items proceed to the next approval level (or procurement queue if final), while rejected line items return to the requester with comments — tracked independently

AC#3 SLA Escalation
GIVEN a PR has been pending an approver&apos;s action for longer than the configured SLA (e.g., 48 hours)
WHEN the SLA breach is detected by the daily job
THEN a reminder notification is sent to the approver; upon a second breach, the PR is escalated to the next-level approver or the approver&apos;s manager

US-03: Creating a Purchase Order from Approved Requests
As a procurement officer, I want to convert one or more approved purchase requests into a purchase order for a selected vendor, So I can ensure every PO I issue is fully traceable to a properly approved request.
This feature will be used on the Procurement Queue and Create Purchase Order screens.

AC#1 PO Creation Restricted to Approved Lines
GIVEN the procurement officer is creating a new PO
WHEN they attempt to add a PR line item to the PO
THEN only PR line items with status &apos;Approved&apos; are selectable; unapproved or rejected lines do not appear in the selection list

AC#2 Consolidating Multiple PRs into One PO
GIVEN multiple approved PRs from different requesters request the same item from the same vendor
WHEN the procurement officer selects line items from more than one PR into a single PO
THEN the PO is created with all selected line items, each retaining a reference back to its originating PR and requester

AC#3 PO Approval Threshold
GIVEN the PO total value exceeds the configured Procurement Manager approval threshold
WHEN the procurement officer submits the PO
THEN the PO enters &apos;Pending PO Approval&apos; status and is routed to the Procurement Manager before it can be issued to the vendor
WHEN the PO total is below the threshold
THEN the PO is issued directly upon submission

US-04: Recording Goods Receipt Against a Purchase Order
As a warehouse or store staff member, I want to record what was physically received against a purchase order, including any discrepancies, So I can make sure the company only pays for what was actually delivered in good condition.
This feature will be used on the Goods Receipt screen, accessible from an open PO.

AC#1 Recording a Goods Receipt
GIVEN the warehouse staff selects an open PO with pending deliveries
WHEN they enter received quantity and condition (Good / Damaged / Rejected) for each line item and submit
THEN a Goods Receipt record is created, cumulative received quantity is updated on the PO, and the PO status updates to &apos;Partially Received&apos; or &apos;Fully Received&apos; accordingly

AC#2 Discrepancy Flagging
GIVEN a goods receipt line item is marked as Damaged or Rejected, or the received quantity differs from ordered quantity beyond tolerance
WHEN the goods receipt is submitted
THEN the GR is flagged as &apos;Discrepancy&apos; and appears on the Procurement and Management dashboards for follow-up

AC#3 Photo Evidence for Damaged Goods
GIVEN a line item is marked as Damaged or Rejected
WHEN the warehouse staff submits the goods receipt
THEN they are prompted (not strictly required, but strongly encouraged via UI) to attach at least one photo as evidence before confirming

US-05: Monitoring Procurement Status Across Branches
As a management or executive user, I want to see a real-time overview of open requests, pending approvals, open purchase orders, and goods receipt status across all branches, So I can identify bottlenecks and spend issues without waiting for someone to compile a report.
This feature will be used on the Management Dashboard, a read-only view accessible to Management and Admin roles.

AC#1 Real-Time Summary Counts
GIVEN the management user opens the dashboard
WHEN the dashboard loads
THEN it displays live counts for: Open PRs, Pending Approvals (by level), Open POs, and Goods Receipts with discrepancies — filterable by branch and date range

AC#2 Drill-Down to Detail
GIVEN the management user is viewing a summary count (e.g., &apos;12 PRs pending approval&apos;)
WHEN they click on that count
THEN a filtered list of the underlying PRs is shown, with the ability to open any individual PR for full detail

AC#3 Aging Alerts
GIVEN a PR has been pending approval beyond its SLA, or a PO is open beyond its expected delivery date
WHEN the dashboard is rendered
THEN these items are visually highlighted (e.g., red indicator) in an &apos;Needs Attention&apos; section at the top of the dashboard



12. Non-Functional Requirements
Role-Based Access Control: Every screen and action is gated by role; a Requester cannot access approval or procurement screens, and vice versa.
Data Segregation with Central Visibility: Branch users see only their branch&apos;s data by default; Management and Admin roles can view across all branches.
Mobile Responsiveness: Approval actions (Approve/Reject) must work well on mobile browsers, since approvers are often away from their desks.
Performance: Approval inbox and dashboard views load within 2 seconds for up to 10,000 concurrent open records.
Audit Log Immutability: Audit log entries are append-only; no user role, including Admin, can edit or delete a logged entry.
Notifications: In-app and email notifications for: PR submitted (to approver), PR approved/rejected (to requester), PO issued (to procurement/requester), goods receipt discrepancy (to procurement).
Attachment Support: PRs support file attachments (quotes, specs) up to 10MB per file; GRs support photo attachments up to 5MB per photo.
Data Retention: All PR, PO, GR, and audit log records are retained indefinitely (or per company&apos;s compliance policy) and remain searchable.

15. Risks & Assumptions
15.1 Risks
Risk
Mitigation
Employees resist moving away from familiar WhatsApp/email habits
Change management: mandatory rollout with a grace period, in-app onboarding, and a clear cutover date after which offline PRs are no longer processed by procurement
Approval matrix misconfiguration causes PRs to route incorrectly
Admin UI includes a matrix preview/test mode before going live; audit log makes misrouting easy to detect and correct
Warehouse staff skip proper GR recording under time pressure
Simplify the GR mobile flow to the fewest possible taps; make GR a prerequisite for PO closure so it can&apos;t be silently skipped
Multiple branches may have inconsistent item naming/pricing
Shared Item Catalog with branch-specific pricing overrides, maintained by Admin, reduces inconsistency over time

15.2 Assumptions
Single currency and single country of operation for this phase; multi-currency is a future consideration.
Each branch has at least one designated Department Approver, Branch Manager, and Warehouse/Store staff member.
The company has (or will define) a formal approval matrix with clear value thresholds — this PRD assumes a 4-tier example matrix, to be confirmed with Finance and Operations stakeholders.
Vendor invoicing and payment (Accounts Payable) are handled by a separate/existing finance process in this phase, outside this module&apos;s scope.
